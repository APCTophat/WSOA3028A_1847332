The medium is the message and now everything is on its head

                    I have had a day to stew on 
                    M. McLuhan, Understanding Media: The Extensions of Man, New York: MIT Press, 1964, pp. 203-209
                    specifically, "The Medium is the message" and all I can say is that my head hurts. I understand what McLuhan 
                    was getting at, I think. From my understanding of the article is that the important question
                    to ask is not "What is being made" but rather "The space in which it is being made in."
                    
                    So, it is not the context of the art piece but rather the entire medium that facilitates the art piece that 
                    conveys the artworks message. This medium is not only the physical medium but also the medium of the
                    cultural, economical, technological and sociological space the piece was created in. And that this artwork
                    conveys its message through or is a reflection of those other mediums. 

                    Which makes sense to me, sort of, I understand the principal that society movies at the rate of its fastest 
                    technology and this rate of progression trickles down into every facit of a society. However, to say that
                    the context of the 'thing', facilitated by the medium, is not the main message being portrayed just baffels me.
                   
                    Looking at the Internet as the current fastest technology and the medium it creates, I understand the importance 
                    of understanding the culture shifting potential this medium has achieved, is achieveing and is yet to achieve.
                    How what work is done in this medium is self reflective on the medium itself. That to understand what message 
                    is being portrayed by the medium is to understand the medium and by extension understand the self which has extended
                    itself through this medium.

                    This reading is really facinating and interesting but man I did not need a whole reshuffle of my understanding 
                    of life right now. 
                    
                    Also, I do not believe McLuhan solved the question of the Chicken and the egg, as I belive his
                    statement can go both ways as well. What if the egg is just the Chicken's idea for getting more Chickens HUH!

Metadata and the book worms that crawl on the web reading it

                    This week we covered the web robots and their function on the internet.
                    As I understand it these robots scower the internet gathing information, this information is used both for good (legitimate) 
                    and evil (malicious).

                    The good robo bros are used to organize/index information on the web so that it can easily be found and cataloged, finds price
                    variations of the same thing from multiple sorces. This allows people to make more informed decisions when buying online. They 
                    are also used to provide a constant source of media to people. Basically all the good robo bros make navigating the web easier
                    for people, and makes traversing the web so much faster.

                    The evil robos operate the same as the good robos, however what information they gather or what the informationis used for is 
                    different. Spam bots collect information, typically contact info like email adresses, about users to spam them with advertising.
                    Hacker web robots try and find vulnerable websites or application so they can be exploited for malicious purposes (side note:
                    shouldn't these robots be called "Cracker web robots" instead then?). Botnets are a network of 'zombie computers' that are used
                    to conduct cordinated online attacks. Download web robots  forcible downloads a specfic web page instead of the requested page.

                    The metadata of a websites is what these robots reads. Each site has its own metadata that is not displayed to the user but rather
                    is displayed for the robots scanning through the web. The metadata is used to describe what is the webpage about or what is 
                    the imortant aspects of the site that wants to be shared. This can be who the author is, what is on the website, the keywords
                    associated with products, ect. The main goal being to make the website as accessable as it needs to be. The metadata specifies
                    who, what and how many people will be able to find the website if it is what they are loking for.
